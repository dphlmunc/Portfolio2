Perfetti Van Melle

Virtual Reality Training

Storydesign, DoP, VR UX-design, video-editing, Unity3D-development

Being one of the world's biggest producers of candies like Mentos, Fruitella and Chupa Chups; Perfetti Van Melle has several factories spread all over the world,
which introduces a few cultural challenges when it comes to training personel in safety and security measures.

In order to gamify this program, I travelled to Shanghai with a crew to film their training using a Jaunt One; the world's most pristine 360-camera at the time.
Prior to shooting, I'd prepared storyboards and callsheets to ensure we'd wrap up in just 2 days of actual filming.
Since I'm no expert on the subject of safety and security, I'd put myself through a crash-course using the original lecture materials, exams, and interviews I'd taken with Perfetti Van Melle's consultants, whom ultimately approved of the script I wrote.



Photography by Sjoerd van Oosten


Upon returning home, I immediately started stitching and editing the footage to make it workable for the VR-app.
While colleagues from Breese were managing the english, italian and mandarin voice acting; me and our colleagues at Sping had started development in Unity3D.
After finishing up editing the voice-overs and releasing V1.0 of Perfetti Van Melle's VR Training, we packaged boxes I'd put together containing instructions on how to assemble and start using the Google Daydream, which were sent all over the world to help PVM's risk & safety personel improve their work right from the comfort of their homes.


You can read more about it on Perfetti Van Melle's website.
